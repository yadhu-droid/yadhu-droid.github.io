THE WASHINGTON POST


This heavy-hitting fist lands with calculated deliberation. Despite Spielberg's obviously genuine commitment, "Schindler's List" feels strangely controlled -- more than impassioned. It's officially artistic, an engineered project of pride, Little Stevie's growing-up project, rather than an organically brilliant masterpiece.

DESSON THOMSON

ROLLING STONE


Schindler's List, despite blatant compromises, is a rending historical document. But the film's near-certain victory is based less on merit than on the marketing of its ambitious intentions. The academy doesn't judge movies, it weighs them by subject matter. On that basis, Spielberg's epic tips the scales.

PETER TRAVERS
EMPIRE

 MY TAKE  

Its one of the best emotional films that i have watched. Steven Spielberg made it in black and white though it was rendered in the 1990s. That black and white touch made it into a film that we admire. Before watching it, I have heard that it was a touching film and that it made some people cry. Few minutes into the movie, I guessed it was another Nazi war film. But I was wrong. I sobbed when it ended.
It was an outstanding movie that would remain as the best film ever made. Also, the fact that it was 90% genuine trembled me. I think that's the reason that I cried too. It has everything to be a perfect film : A very powerful storyline, Direction, Cinematography, Actor's Performance, Visual Editing, Background Score, Production Design and everything.
The emotions were strongly portrayed as well. The use of simbolism by the director tells a lot about the theme of the movie. It is a must watch and you wont be disappointed
